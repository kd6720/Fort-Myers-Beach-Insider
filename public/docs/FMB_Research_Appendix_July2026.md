# Fort Myers Beach — Research Appendix (July 2026)
### Supporting data for the 2026 Investment Prospectus · Prepared for Carter Dewey

## 1. Infrastructure Status

- **Estero Blvd:** $9M Ian-repair program (Wright Construction), ~25% complete July 2026, target Fall 2027. Covers asphalt, drainage, sidewalks, ADA, pedestrian signals.
- **San Carlos Blvd / Matanzas Pass corridor:** $13M FDOT project complete — new Main St signal, bike lanes, widened bridge path, smart signals, seasonal (Jan–Apr) bus/bike transit lane.
- **Beach renourishment:** COMPLETE Aug 2025 — ~$23M, ~1M cubic yards (Ahtna Marine). Follow-on: $510K dune planting (100K+ plants), new truck-haul sand project started May 2026.
- **Utilities:** water/sewer fully restored; FEMA CRS Class 5 rating (2026) = 25% flood-insurance discounts island-wide.
- **Civic:** new storm-hardened Town Hall opened Sept 2025 ($7M purchase, ~$10M saved vs new build); library reopened Mar 2025; Bay Oaks Rec Campus reopened Jun 2025.
- **Transit/marine:** free seasonal trolley + tram restored; mooring field reopened (19 moorings, vessels to 85 ft).

## 4. Housing Market (mid-2026)

- **Median sale:** $546K–$575K depending on source (Houzeo/Zillow/RealtyTrac); down 9–18% YoY. 95% of sales close below list; sale-to-list 0.934.
- **Segments:** single-family median $1.23M · condo median $579K · market avg $665/sq ft · new elevated construction $1,100–$1,300/sq ft.
- **Inventory:** ~400 active home listings + 209 vacant lots (median $745K; gulf-front $2.7–3.0M; bay/canal $550K–$1.8M). 11–13 months supply; 97–157 days on market; lots avg 237 DOM.
- **Condo headwinds:** milestone inspections + SIRS reserve mandates (deadline Dec 31, 2025); special assessments $20K–$100K+; 63.4% cash purchases (non-warrantable buildings); HOA fees to ~$2K/month.
- **Insurance:** legacy VE-zone flood $5K–$20K+/yr vs materially cheaper on new elevated builds; Lee County wind avg ~$6,180; Citizens filed first rate DECREASE (−2.6% avg) effective June 2026. FEMA 50% rule drives teardown/rebuild economics.
- **New construction:** all-concrete on pilings, 15–20 ft elevation; six elevated homes under construction in the 700 block of Estero alone (Feb 2026); realtors report shortage of new-construction resale inventory.

## 5. Hotel & Vacation Rental Inventory

- **Hotels:** pre-Ian ~2,000 rooms / 43 properties → ~59% back online mid-2026. Operating: Margaritaville (254), Pink Shell (195 units, 80%+ occupancy), Lani Kai (125), DiamondHead (~124), Best Western Plus (74), GullWing, Pointe Estero, Edison Beach House, Lovers Key Resort, Gulfview Manor (33). 19–21 small hotels destroyed; only 2 rebuilt.
- **Tourism:** Lee County FY24-25: 3.27M visitors (+4.3%), $3.20B direct spend, 4.60M room nights. Bed tax FY25 $46.8M (+5.8%); early FY26 +20.9% YoY. County ADR $168 (+10.2%) — supply-constrained pricing power. Town taxable value $4.94B (2025), above pre-Ian.
- **STRs:** ADR $370–$425; occupancy 39–57% (vendor-dependent); avg listing grosses $33K–$46K/yr; 5BR homes ~$166K/yr. Supply surging (+19% to +72% YoY by vendor) as elevated new builds come online.
- **STR rules:** registration mandatory (<180-day rentals), ~$300/yr, 24/7 local contact, annual fire inspection. Weekly rentals allowed in Gulf-side exception zone; monthly minimum in residential zones. Enforcement surge: ~612–650 unregistered units identified; violation notices began Jan 2026. Taxes: ~11%+ combined (state + county + 5% TDT).

- Gross STR yields 8–9% market-wide; net compresses hard on legacy stock (insurance, 20–30% management, taxes, sub-57% occupancy). New elevated builds in the walkable north end fix both the insurance and occupancy sides.
- Hotel comps: Margaritaville ~$787K/key development cost; JW Marriott Marco Island traded ~$1.03M/key (May 2026).
- **Outlook:** Realtor.com forecasts Cape Coral–Fort Myers −10.2% for 2026 (steepest US metro) — tactical buying window. Structural bull case: fixed island land supply, 41% lodging deficit, completed beach, pier delivery 2027-28, hardened housing stock, RSW expansion.
- **Risks:** pier permitting timing (USACE), Seagate/Red Coconut uncertainty, insurance volatility, hurricane exposure, Nov 2026 council election could shift development posture.

## 7. RSW Airport Expansion (The Demand Pipeline)

- **Program:** ~$1.7B total terminal expansion — largest capital program in Lee County Port Authority history. Funded via a $725M bond sale (approved 5-0, Jan 2026) repaid by airline fees and rental-car charges — no local tax dollars.
- **Concourse E:** new 14-gate concourse (infrastructure for 19), ~$1.1B, Suffolk Construction; hit its halfway point Dec 2025; targeted completion by end of 2027. Site reserved for a future fifth concourse.
- **Phase 1:** consolidated 16-lane TSA checkpoint + 281K sq ft remodeled/new space; re-baselined to ~$654–678M with completion March 2029 (present the program + Concourse E as the growth story; Phase 1 is not yet open).
- **Traffic records:** 2023: 10.07M → 2024: 11.03M → **2025: 11.15M — busiest year in airport history**; Oct 2025 monthly record 774,689 (+18% YoY). Top-50 U.S. airport; second-busiest single-runway airport in the U.S. 16 airlines; nonstops across the U.S., Canada, and Frankfurt.
- **Economic impact:** $8.3B annual output, ~60,500 jobs, $2.7B payroll (FDOT study). Page Field private aviation ops +5% YoY.
- **Beach relevance:** the TDC explicitly cites RSW expansion as a demand catalyst for the island's supply-constrained lodging market.

## 8. Greater Fort Myers / Lee County Growth

- **Population:** Lee County 875,607 (July 2025), **+15.1% since 2020** — among America's fastest-growing large counties. Median household income $76,107.
- **Babcock Ranch:** record 1,066 net home sales in 2025 (+34%), 15,000+ residents, top-5 best-selling U.S. master-planned community (RCLCO/John Burns); hurricane resilience (never lost power in Ian) drives demand.
- **Lee Health Fort Myers:** $820M, 236-bed hospital campus at Colonial Blvd — SWFL's largest-ever healthcare build; topped out; opening early 2028; further $150.5M/92-bed expansion approved.
- **Roads:** Big Carlos Pass Bridge opened Nov 2024 (full completion mid-2026); Cape Coral Bridge widening 4→6 lanes and SR 31 six-laning start construction 2026.
- **Anchors:** FGCU 16,633 students (~half of alumni stay in SWFL); Red Sox (JetBlue Park) + Twins (Lee Health Sports Complex) spring training; Cape Coral's $125M Bimini Square opened Feb 2026.
- **Jobs:** metro nonfarm +0.7% YE June 2025; construction the standout at +2.6%.

## 9. Downtown Fort Myers (River District)

- **Master plan:** 60+ acres of downtown/Midtown redevelopment over five years, estimated **$6B investment**.
- **News-Press site (10 ac):** 350 residences, 150K sq ft office, 50K sq ft retail, 140–160-room hotel; construction starts Dec 2026, completion 2029.
- **City of Palms Park (21 ac):** mixed-use destination; stadium demolition early 2027.
- **Yacht Basin:** Suntex Marinas — up to 280 slips, riverwalk, 40K sq ft dining/retail; USACE approval expected from summer 2026.
- **New proposal (Jan 2026):** 23-story, up-to-600-unit riverfront hotel/residential tower near Edison & Ford estates (zoning pending).
- **Delivered anchors:** Luminary Hotel ($91M, 243 rooms, Autograph Collection) + Caloosa Sound Convention Center & Amphitheater.
- **Residential pipeline (1,700+ units):** Silver Hills/Triton Cay (327 riverfront apartments, Phase I occupied), Vantage Lofts (217), Prima Luce (220 waterfront condos, foundation underway), West End at City Walk (318 leased + Phase II), Palmera on the River (221), First Street Apartments (274), Montage at Midtown (321), plus three hotels in the pipeline.
- **Scene:** monthly Art Walk (18th year) and Music Walk; Edison & Ford Winter Estates; reopened Legacy Harbour Marina. 20 minutes from Fort Myers Beach — deepens the region's year-round demand base.
